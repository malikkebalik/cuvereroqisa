Video Viral: Attempted Stabbing of Mosque Imam in Jakarta Timur

A video depicting a man allegedly attempting to harm a mosque imam in Jakarta Timur has gone viral on social media. The video, which was uploaded on the Instagram account @kabarjaktim on Saturday (16/12/2023), shows a man wearing a green shirt being apprehended by locals after being suspected of planning a stabbing attack. Despite being captured, the man remained silent and did not resist. The incident took place at Musala Baitul Huda on Batu Ampar I Street, RT 17/RW 02, Batu Ampar Village, Kramat Jati District, Jakarta Timur, on Friday (15/12/2023) at around 7:40 PM.

Incident Details

The mosque’s imam, Lazuardy Firdaus, revealed that he was almost stabbed by a man identified as MMA (26) after leading the congregational Isya prayer. “As I was leaving the mosque to start my motorcycle, the perpetrator suddenly approached me and asked, ‘Are you the imam?’ I replied, ‘Yes,'” Lazuardy said in an interview in Jakarta Timur on Saturday (16/12/2023), as quoted from TribunJakarta.com. MMA, who had only been renting in the Batu Ampar area for about a month, then took out a kitchen knife from his pants pocket and attempted to stab the imam. Fortunately, Lazuardy managed to dodge the attack and subdue MMA. “I only sustained minor injuries, nothing serious. I managed to evade the attack and fell off my motorcycle. I shouted for help, and the locals immediately came to my aid. They apprehended the perpetrator here at the mosque,” Lazuardy explained.

Imam’s Account of the Incident

According to Lazuardy, the perpetrator claimed that he was disturbed by the sound of the mosque’s speaker. However, Lazuardy emphasized that the incident was unexpected and unprovoked. He expressed gratitude for escaping the attack with minor injuries and praised the swift response of the locals in apprehending the perpetrator. Lazuardy’s account of the incident highlights the importance of maintaining a safe and peaceful environment within religious institutions.

Arrest and Investigation

Following the incident, MMA was taken into custody by the Bhabinkamtibmas personnel of Batu Ampar Village and brought to the Kramat Jati Police Station for further investigation. Kramat Jati Police Chief, Kompol Rusit Malaka, stated that during preliminary questioning, MMA admitted to carrying out the attack due to being disturbed by the mosque’s speaker. He explained that MMA lived in close proximity to the mosque and decided to take a kitchen knife with him to confront the imam. MMA is now being held at the Kramat Jati Police Station as a suspect and is charged under Article 335 of the Indonesian Penal Code, which pertains to the use of force. Additionally, he is also charged under Article 2 of Emergency Law No. 12 of 1951 regarding the possession of sharp weapons, which carries a maximum penalty of 10 years in prison. The investigation is ongoing, with the involvement of witnesses and the attention of the Kapolres (Chief of Police).
