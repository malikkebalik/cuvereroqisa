As an understudy, Philip Casnoff started his acting career. Afterwards, he received a role in the Broadway production Rockabye Hamlet. While only running for seven performances, this piece was an adaptation of a rock musical by William Shakespeare.

He subsequently received a role in the miniseries-based musical Shogun. He was hurt during this time but quickly made a full recovery. After this in 200, Philip returned to the broadways to play Billy Flynn in the musical Chicago.

His most significant achievement was included in the miniseries North and South, which had two sequels. In this series, he portrayed Elkanah Bent. In addition, the series debuted in 1985. Subsequently, he received the role of Frank Sinatra in the television movie Sinatra. He personally met Sinatra in order to make his act more sincere and realistic.

He was nominated for a Golden Globe for this performance. In the miniseries Oz, he had a standout performance as Nikolai Stanislofsky, a Russian gangster. He then had a five-season role in Strong Medicine.
