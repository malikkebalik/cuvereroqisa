Michelle Williams' Husbands and Boyfriends Over the Years

 Posted on August 3, 2024  |  4 minutes  |  727 words  |  Reinaldo Massengill

Explore Michelle Williams' personal life through the years: The actress has been married twice!  

Best known for her award-winning roles in Brokeback Mountain, Blue Valentine, Fosse/Verdon, The Fabelsman, and Manchester By Sea, Michelle Williams is a celebrated actress. With a career repertoire as stellar as any of her male contemporaries, including her former co-star Leonardo DiCaprio, Williams is a certified powerhouse in Hollywood.  

Born on September 9, 1980, in Kalispell, Montana, a few in history have been as sure, and courageous as Williams, who, at 15, filed for emancipation from her parents so that she could focus on her acting career without breaking the child labor law. The actress, 42, today is also an ardent advocate for various social issues, including sexism, the gender pay gap, and more.  

Inside Michelle Williams's Romantic History! 

Alongside her illustrious acting career, Michelle has also been navigating a high-profile personal life. The Oscar-nominated actress's associations with some A-list stars have constantly brought her attention, often overwhelming, and inundated. Michelle wasn't the magnificent movie star that she is today when she started dating Jeremy Jackson in the 90s.   

Williams' relationship with Jeremy, which reportedly lasted two years, was so under-covered that the press only discovered it when Jackson made the news public knowledge in 2011. The Blue Valentine actress famously dated her Brokeback Mountain co-star, Heath Ledger.  

Michelle and Ledger's relationship transpired to be a hasty one, with the pair announcing their engagement, and first pregnancy after months of dating. The tabloid's favorite couple welcomed their first child, Matilda Ledger, in October 2005, and called it quits after two years in 2007- just months before Ledger's tragic passing at the age of 28.   

Michelle became the subject of massive media scrutiny following the death of Ledger. Some even assumed the actress's role in the Oscar winner's demise. Overwhelmed by the perpetual media attention, Williams moved to upstate New York in search of a quieter life.  

In the years following Heath's death, Williams was linked with directors Spike Jonze and Cary Joji Fukunaga, actor Jason Segel, and artist Dustin Yellin. The five-time Oscar-nominated actress was briefly engaged to financier Andrew Youmans in 2018.   

Michelle surprised the world in July 2018 when she revealed that she had secretly tied the knot with musician Phil Elverum, who was a widower after his first wife Geneviève Castrée, died in 2016. The couple reportedly said their vows in a private ceremony in the Adirondack Mountains in July 2018. But the notoriously private couple's marriage ended as swiftly as it had started.    

Williams co-parented her daughter, Matilda, with Oscar-winner Heath Ledger until his death in 2007! 

The Fabelsman actress and her musician husband had called it quits by April 2019. Although the reason behind their split was never revealed, Williams spoke highly of Elverum during their time together. "I'm finally loved by someone who makes me feel free," the actress had said.  

Inside Michelle Williams' Second Conjugal Life with Thomas Kail!    

After weathering intense media scrutiny and speculations in the past, Williams has learned her lesson to keep it quiet, and private. The acclaimed actress is going strong with her second husband, Thomas Kail, a theatre director. Williams and Kail's relationship has also been a whirlwind affair.  

The Montana-born actress met Kail when she played the legendary dancer Gwen Verdon in Thomas's directorial, Fosse/Verdon, in late 2018. And before fans and media could witness the duo slowly translate their relationship into real life, Williams and Kail had already been married. The pair reportedly got hitched in March 2020; a year after Thomas divorced his first wife, actress Angela Christian.   

The low-key couple welcomed their firstborn, Williams' second, son Hart, in June 2020. The twosome became a family of five when the Shutter Island actress gave birth to a baby, whose name is kept discreet at the moment, in late 2022. "There's nothing that makes you committed to a better world than raising a great kid. It's the ultimate creative act," the actress said about motherhood.  

Michelle's lesser-known husband, Kail, 45, is an acclaimed theatre director. Thomas is best known for his working closely with Lin-Manuel Miranda in the Heights and Hamilton. A Tony-winning director, Kail's notable credits also include Grease Live, Tiny Beautiful Things, and Magic/Bird.
