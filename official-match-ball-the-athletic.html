Scaloni and Infantino arrive

Argentina manager Lionel Scaloni and FIFA president Gianni Infantino arrived back-to-back within a minute of each other.

The crowd around Scaloni was nuts, with fans and media alike crowding around him as he made his way to the red carpet. Infantino’s car pulled up seconds later and he emerged and headed inside.

Berhalter: Copa America is like a 'mini World Cup'

(Photo: Getty Images)

USMNT manager Gregg Berhalter spoke to Fox Sports ahead of the draw: "It's a very prestigious tournament. This is the 46th edition of Copa America. Some very competitive South American teams on our home soil so for us it's like a mini World Cup.

"It's about us continuing to improve. We showed we can compete on the world stage and now, how do we win on the world stage, that's the next test. When you look at our group, it's a really solid group of guys. All of them are progressing with their clubs, and now we need to take the next step together.

"We really need to focus on that core group of players and keep moving them along. I'm not sure Copa America is the time to experiment with a lot of new players. But we had a young group at the last World Cup so this group is more than ready to take part in the Copa America and then the 2026 World Cup."

Former USSF president Carlos Cordeiro on hand too

Former USSF president Carlos Cordeiro, now a senior advisor to FIFA president Gianni Infantino is here tonight. Cordeiro lives here in Miami, where FIFA is moving its headquarters.

Cordeiro resigned as USSF president in 2020 amid a backlash to court filings related to the equal pay lawsuit with the U.S. women’s national team.

A former Copa America winner is here

(Photo: Getty Images)

Diego Godin, who won the Copa America with Uruguay in 2011, is on hand for tonight's draw after retiring from football earlier this year.

Messi set to break Copa America appearances record

Lionel Messi is set to break the record for the most appearances in Copa America history in Argentina's first match — which is the opening game of the tournament in Atlanta on June 20.

The match will be his 35th Copa America appearance, which will surpass former Chile goalkeeper Sergio Livingstone's total. That record has stood since 1953!
