Who is Patti LuPone

Patti LuPone is an American actress, comedian, singer, and voice artist. She is best known for her role as Elizabeth “Libby” Thatcher on ABC series, Life Goes On from 1989 to 1993.

LuPone recently wrapped her role in an upcoming young adult fantasy film, The School for Good and Evil. It is set to be released in 2022 on Netflix.

Her other upcoming project is the comedy horror film, Disappointment Blvd.

What is the full name of Patti LuPone?

Patti LuPone was born on April 21, 1949, in Northport, New York, the U.S. Her full name is Patti Ann LuPone.

She is the daughter of Angela Louise Lupone (mother) and Orland Joseph LuPone (father). Her father is a school administrator and English teacher at Walt Whitman High School in Huntington, Long Island.

Similarly, Patti LuPone’s mother is a library administrator at the C.W. Post Campus of Long Island University.

She has twin brothers named Robert LuPone and William LuPone. Her older brother, Robert is also a Tony-nominated actor, dancer, and director whereas William is an Ecologist.

Patti LuPone is of American nationality and belongs to a mixed (Italian-American and Roman Catholic) ethnicity.

Education

Patti LuPone went to Juilliard’s Drama Division in Manhattan, New York where she was part of the first graduating class of Juilliard’s Drama Division.

She was a classmate of fellow actors Kevin Kline and David Ogden Stiers.

In 1972, she graduated from Juilliard with a Bachelor of Fine Arts degree.

Patti LuPone: Professional Life, Career

Acting career

Patti LuPone started her acting career as a theater artist in Odd-Broadway, The School for Scandals where she played the character of Lady Teazle.

She was also a member of The Acting Company where she performed in numerous productions such as The Cradle Will Rock, The School for Scandals, Women Beware Women, The Hostage, and more.

Patti LuPone then made her first TV appearance on the crime drama series The Andros Targets as Sharon Walker in 1977.

The following year, she made her theatrical film debut in an uncredited role in the drama film King of the Gypsies.

Her notable movies are Witness (1985), Driving Miss Daisy (1989), and Mamet’s Heist (2001). Similarly, she guest-starred in TV series like Law & Order, Will & Grace, Ugly Betty, Oz, 30 Rock, and so on.

Other ventures

Patti LuPone is also popular for her mezzo-soprano vocal range, performed in numerous New York concert productions of musicals.

Her performance in Juli Covington’s song, “Don’t Cary for Me Argentina” at the Grammy Awards was released on the 1994 album Grammy’s Greatest Moments Volume IV.

In July 2012, her live concert special film, An Evening with Patti LuPone was filmed and released in November 2012 on SerhTv. com hosted by Seth Rudetsky.

Some of her songs include Patti LuPone Live (Solo Album), Matters of the Heart (Solo Album), Far Away Places (Solo Album), Company (New York Philharmonic recording), War Paint (Original Broadway cast recording), Don’t Monkey with Broadway (Solo Album).

Aside from this, she has written a memoir titled ‘Patti LuPone: A Memoir’ which recounts her life and career from childhood to the present. The book was published in September 2010.
